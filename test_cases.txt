A city library system wants to use AI to predict which families are at risk of “low early literacy engagement” based on library card activity, children’s event attendance, zip code, overdue fines, and school district data. The goal is to proactively offer free reading kits, text-message nudges, and librarian outreach before kindergarten. Leadership believes this will improve school readiness, but staff worry it may stigmatize families, miss households without library cards, and redirect resources away from universal programs.

A large hospital system wants to deploy an AI model in emergency departments to predict which patients are at high risk of returning within 72 hours after discharge. The goal is to identify patients who should receive additional testing, follow-up calls, or observation before leaving the hospital. The model would use electronic health records, prior admissions, medications, insurance status, housing instability indicators, and demographic information. Hospital leadership expects the system to reduce readmissions and improve patient outcomes, but clinicians worry the model may reinforce existing healthcare disparities, increase unnecessary testing, and reduce physician autonomy. There are also concerns that the model could become a liability if clinicians disagree with its recommendations.

A mid-sized software company is considering replacing its traditional annual performance reviews with an AI-powered continuous feedback system. The AI would analyze project contributions, peer feedback, meeting participation, and code quality to generate performance insights throughout the year. Leadership believes this will make evaluations more objective and timely, while employees worry about privacy, surveillance, bias, and being judged by incomplete data. The company must decide whether and how to proceed.

A mid-sized city is considering removing a concrete flood channel and restoring it to a natural river ecosystem with wetlands, native vegetation, and public green spaces. Environmental groups argue the project will improve biodiversity, water quality, and long-term climate resilience. Local businesses and residents worry about increased flooding, mosquitoes, construction disruption, and the cost of maintaining the restored ecosystem. The city council must decide whether to proceed, modify the proposal, or reject it.
